What is handball ?

Handball is a fast-paced team sport where two teams of seven players each aim to score goals by throwing a small ball into the opposing team's net. Played on a court similar in size to a basketball court, handball combines elements of soccer and basketball, requiring both physical endurance and strategic teamwork. Each game consists of two halves, typically lasting 30 minutes each. Players pass, dribble, and shoot to outmaneuver defenders, all while following specific rules to avoid fouls. Known for its high-scoring nature and dynamic gameplay, handball is particularly popular in Europe, where professional leagues and international tournaments attract a large fan base.
